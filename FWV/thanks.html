    15. Whom would you like to thank for teaching you Respect?

      Albert Fernandez: With such an immoral world nowadays, the principle of respect is one of the key values maintaining society in balance. To obtain this value, we all need a lesson. My lesson in particular is owned to a play that I watched not too long ago titled, “12 Angry Men.” In this play, the fate of a man accused of murder depended on the unanimous decisions of the jury. At first, eleven jury men declare the man guilty within five minutes and were ready to sentence him to death. One of them however, decided to consider the facts again to at least show interest for his life. As actual discussion was open on the case, reasonable doubts arose resulting in the unanimous vote of innocence for the “criminal”. Respect does not comes from proving a “criminal” to be innocent, but the example of the jury man who wanted to go deeper into the case is what taught me respect. It was these 12 actors representing the justice of a system such as the one in the U.S that showed me respect. The willingness to consider the life of a criminal to me showed something so humane that I immediately accepted it. These men showed respect in the darkest of situations, and such was their respect and compassion that I felt changed by this play to show respect in everyday life. To these 12 men and especially to the man who opened the discussion, dignity for this man was present at all times and the respect for his life was simply beyond explanation in my eyes. I walked out of the theatre thinking “if there is respect for those who seem not to deserve it, then why should I be disrespectful?” Until this day, I have not yet found an answer.
      12 days ago
